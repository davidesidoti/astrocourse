Stellar Formation Simulator

This interactive simulation demonstrates the process of stellar formation from a molecular cloud. Adjust the parameters to see how they affect the formation process.

What to observe:

  • Higher density and lower temperature lead to faster collapse
  • The red circle shows the Jeans radius - if it's smaller than the cloud, the cloud is stable
  • Higher rotation rates lead to larger accretion disks
  • Bipolar outflows appear when rotation is significant

What to observe:

  • Low-mass stars (<0.3 M☉) are fully convective
  • Sun-like stars have radiative cores and convective envelopes
  • Massive stars have convective cores and radiative envelopes
  • Temperature, density, and pressure all decrease from center to surface
